Current Research Activities

AI2Equity (NIH)

National Institutes of Health · Apr 2024 – Mar 2028
Qualitative Modified Delphi Cardiovascular equity Patients + providers

This qualitative sub-study within the AI2Equity initiative examines how patients and healthcare providers perceive cardiovascular risk, access to care, and the role of AI-enabled tools in promoting equitable prevention and treatment.

Findings directly inform the design and evaluation of equitable AI-based cardiovascular risk tools by integrating multi-stakeholder perspectives into model development and implementation.

SaTC:EDU – Cybersecurity Training for Healthcare Professionals (NSF)

National Science Foundation · Aug 2023 – Jul 2026
Cybersecurity Instructional design Healthcare workforce Pre/post evaluation

This project supports the development, deployment, and evaluation of cybersecurity training for next-generation healthcare professionals.

Work includes coordinating instructional delivery across learning platforms, managing course logistics, and supporting the collection and analysis of pre- and post-training assessment data.

DREAM-KG / Proto-OKN (NSF)

National Science Foundation · Oct 2023 – Sep 2026
Knowledge graphs Explainable AI Community engagement Human-centered design

DREAM-KG develops explainable, adaptive knowledge graphs to address homelessness through AI-enabled decision support.

Contributions focus on coordinating the Community Advisory Board and supporting processes that integrate community perspectives into technical development to ensure human-centered, equitable system design.

Research Methods & Interests

Research interests and methods include qualitative and mixed-methods study design, community-engaged research, modified Delphi approaches, focus groups, IRB protocol development, evaluation of training and educational interventions, and the application of AI and social determinants of health to advance equity in public health systems.
